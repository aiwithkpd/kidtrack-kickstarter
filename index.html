Why We Built This

Toddlers move fast. Peace of mind shouldn't cost a subscription.

Every parent of a toddler knows the feeling — you look away for two seconds and they've vanished around a corner. Existing trackers are either locked behind expensive monthly plans, packed with bloatware, or designed for older kids. None of them are open, hackable, or truly built for the 1–4 year age group.

"I wanted to know when my 18-month-old fell asleep, how long he napped, and whether he was still in the backyard. Nothing on the market did all three without a monthly fee."

KidTrack is our answer: a fully open-source, DIY-friendly wearable that gives you GPS location, motion-based activity detection, and sleep monitoring — all synced to your phone via Bluetooth, zero subscription required.

How It Works

Four systems working together.

1

GPS tracks location

The MAX-M8Q GPS module receives satellite signals and sends NMEA position sentences to the nRF52840 over UART at configurable intervals. Even indoors, the low-power GPS maintains a last-known fix.

2

IMU detects motion & sleep

The LSM6DS3TR-C 6-axis IMU streams accelerometer and gyroscope data at up to 416Hz over I²C. Firmware classifies motion patterns: active play, walking, stillness, and sleep — no cloud processing needed, all on-device.

3

BLE broadcasts to your phone

The nRF52840 advertises a BLE GATT service with characteristic UUIDs for location, activity state, battery level, and sleep log. Any BLE-capable phone can connect. The open specification lets you build custom dashboards or automations.

4

Battery & charging management

The 500mAh 3.7V LiPo plugs directly into the nRF52840's onboard JST connector. The USB-C breakout handles 5V input; the MCU's built-in charger circuit handles the LiPo. Battery percentage is monitored on the A0 analog pin and surfaced over BLE.

Frequently Asked Questions

Got questions?

What age range is KidTrack designed for?
KidTrack was designed from the ground up for toddlers 1 year and older. The enclosure size (40×40×15mm) and the adjustable silicone strap fit wrists and ankles in the 10–16cm circumference range typical for ages 1–5. For older children, the longer 20×220mm strap fits wrists up to 18cm comfortably.
Does it require a monthly subscription or internet connection?
No subscription, ever. KidTrack syncs data to your phone over Bluetooth Low Energy — no server, no cloud, no ongoing cost. The GPS module receives satellite signals directly; no cellular plan is needed. The only connectivity requirement is that your phone is within BLE range (~10–30 meters) to receive live data. If it goes out of range, the device logs data locally and syncs when reconnected.
How accurate is the GPS tracking?
The u-blox MAX-M8Q provides 1–2 meter CEP accuracy in open-sky conditions, which is excellent for outdoor tracking in parks, backyards, and playgrounds. Indoor accuracy degrades as GPS signals cannot penetrate ceilings; in those cases the device maintains a last-known fix and uses IMU dead-reckoning to estimate position until satellite lock is reacquired.
How long does the battery last?
Runtime depends heavily on GPS polling interval. At the default setting (position fix every 5 seconds), expect approximately 18–24 hours. Reducing GPS to once-per-30-seconds extends runtime to 3–4 days. The battery percentage is always visible via BLE and will trigger a low-battery alert at 20%. Charging from empty to full via USB-C takes roughly 2 hours.
Is the enclosure waterproof?
The PETG enclosure with M2 screw-fastened lid provides splash resistance suitable for rain and hand-washing. It is not rated for submersion. The current design does not include a gasket seal; a stretch goal improvement adds a silicone O-ring to the lid for IPX4 splash resistance. Avoid submerging the device in water or leaving it in puddles.
Do I need to know how to code to use KidTrack?
For the Ready-to-Wear tier: no. The MCU ships pre-flashed and the BLE GATT server is live out of the box. Any BLE scanner app can read the data immediately. A reference companion app (iOS/Android) is a stretch goal at $25k. For the DIY Kit and Blueprint tiers, you'll need basic Arduino/PlatformIO experience to compile and flash firmware — full documentation and step-by-step guides are included.
What tools do I need to build the DIY kit?
You'll need: a soldering iron with a fine tip, solder, wire strippers, flush cutters, a multimeter, an M2 screwdriver, and a heat gun (or lighter) for the heat-set inserts. The 3D-printed enclosure comes pre-printed — no 3D printer required for the kit tier. You'll also need a computer with Arduino IDE or PlatformIO installed for the initial firmware flash.
Is this safe for children — are the materials tested?
PETG (the enclosure material) is food-safe and BPA-free, widely used in medical device housings. The silicone strap is hypoallergenic and non-irritating. The M2 screws are internal and inaccessible to small hands. KidTrack uses Bluetooth Low Energy (BLE), which emits far less RF radiation than a typical smartphone (SAR < 0.1 W/kg). The device is not CE/FCC certified in this Kickstarter form — certification is a stretch goal.
