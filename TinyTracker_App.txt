Based on the product details for KidTrack (the open-hardware toddler safety watch built around the nRF52840 MCU, MAX-M8Q GPS, and LSM6DS3TR-C IMU), the companion mobile app needs to be highly optimized for local, Bluetooth-only (BLE) communication (unless the cellular stretch goal is met) and focused on real-time, zero-subscription data visualization.

Here is a comprehensive list of features categorized into core domains:

1. Real-Time Tracking & Mapping (Core Navigation)
Because this is a BLE-centric tracker, the app needs to smartly handle proximity, offline maps, and last-known positions.

Proximity Map View: A live map showing the parent’s phone location relative to the kid's watch.

Offline Maps / Saching: Since the device operates without a cellular network, parents may use it in parks or campsites without internet. The app should allow downloading local maps.

Last-Known Location (LKL) Pin: If the child goes out of the ~30-meter BLE range, the app must immediately drop a pin on the map marking the exact GPS coordinates where the signal was lost.

IMU Dead-Reckoning Visualizer: When indoors (where GPS degrades), the app can use data from the 6-axis IMU to show an estimated path or vector of where the toddler moved after losing satellite lock.

2. Smart Alerts & Safety (Proactive Monitoring)
Virtual BLE Tether (Geofencing): An adjustable "digital leash" (e.g., 10m, 20m, 30m). If the BLE signal strength drops below a certain threshold (the child is wandering too far), the phone triggers a loud, distinct alarm.

Reconnection Alert: A subtle, reassuring notification when the child comes back into BLE range.

Tamper/Removal Detection: Using sudden spikes in the IMU (gyroscope/accelerometer) data or rapid temperature shifts to alert the parent if the watch is forcefully pulled off or dropped.

3. Health, Activity & Sleep Analytics
The hardware features an advanced 6-axis IMU that classifies motion patterns directly on-device. The app should turn this raw telemetry into actionable metrics:

Activity Metrics Dashboard:

Step-Equivalent Counter: Translates toddler shuffling/running into an easy-to-read daily step count.

Active Play vs. Stillness Time: A ring or pie chart showing hours spent running/playing versus sitting.

Automated Sleep & Nap Logger: * Because the watch auto-detects sleep without button-pressing, the app should feature a Sleep History Calendar.

Shows total overnight sleep duration, nap times (morning vs. afternoon), and sleep quality (restlessness based on micro-movements).

"Toddler Awake" Push Notification: Triggers a silent or quiet alert on the parent's phone the moment the IMU registers wakeful movement after a nap.

4. Device Management & Power Metrics
Battery management is crucial since runtime varies between 18 hours and 4 days depending on how aggressive the GPS tracking is.

Dynamic Battery Life Indicator: Displays exact battery percentage (via the A0 analog pin telemetry).

Battery-to-Time Calculator: Displays estimated remaining battery in hours based on current usage patterns.

Adaptive GPS Polling Toggle: A slider in the app allowing parents to trade battery for accuracy:

High-Security Mode: 5-second interval updates (lasts ~18–24 hours, best for crowded parks).

Battery-Saver Mode: 30-second intervals (lasts 3–4 days, best for a weekend at home).

Low Battery Warn System: Sends a critical notification to the phone when the watch drops to 20% and 10%.

5. Open-Source & Developer-Friendly Features
Since KidTrack brands itself as an open-source, hackable, zero-subscription community product, the app should embrace this audience.

Raw Telemetry Stream (Dev Mode): A toggleable console view displaying real-time NMEA GPS sentences, raw X/Y/Z accelerometer metrics, and BLE RSSI signal strength.

OTA (Over-The-Air) Firmware Updates: The ability to flash updated firmware versions or community-made custom watch scripts onto the nRF52840 MCU via BLE directly from the app.

Data Export: A button to export the child’s location history, sleep logs, and activity metrics into standard formats (.CSV, .GPX, .JSON) so parents can map it in Google Earth or store it locally.

6. Multi-Device & Profile Management
Multi-Child Dashboard: Allows a parent to pair and swap between multiple KidTrack watches simultaneously if they have more than one child.

Co-Parent Sync (Local Link): The ability to securely authorize a second phone (e.g., the other parent or a babysitter) to pair with the BLE profile, turning whichever phone is closest into the active tracking terminal.
